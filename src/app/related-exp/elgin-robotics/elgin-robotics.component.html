Founder of Elgin Robotics (FTC 2016-2018)

I started the Elgin Robotics club with one of my friend back in grade 9. Our team had participated in the FTC (First Tech Challenge for 3 consecutive years).

You can learn more about our team and the FTC through our website at https://elginrobotics.com and https://www.firstinspires.org/

Above was a picture we took during the first year's FTC competition, while our team only had 4 members.

Outside of the competition, we also found lots of community outreach opportunities.

Below was a picture of me riding on an FRC robot (First Robotics Competition, also a FIRST program) during the British Columbia Teachers' Convention.

During the event, we demonstrated what our robot could do to the younger audiences and explained to them what FIRST is. Our purpose was to get more kids who showed interest towards engineering involved into programs like FIRST, at the same time inspire them with more innovational ideas.

We also participated in Surrey City government community programs like AAS (Adopt-A-Street).

Where our club adopted a street besides our school to maintain once everyweek, picking up litter, looking for obstacles that might cause troubes for the pedestrians, etc.

We also get to put two of these AAS signs on the street, I still feel like they look pretty cool everytime I drove by them.
